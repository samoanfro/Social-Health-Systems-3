The Reframe

This isn't just a mental health
crisis. It's a social health crisis.

Mental health is the most visible symptom. But beneath it lies something deeper — and largely unaddressed: the quality, frequency, and infrastructure of human connection itself. Social Health is the missing pillar of well-being. It is the soil. Without it, everything else fails to grow.

The Framework

Connection is infrastructure.

Four foundational practices that turn culture from compliance into connection. Not abstract values — operational layers of every healthy organization.

01 / Foundation
Connection Before Concern
See people as humans to regard before problems to solve. Trust and empathy come first — before correction, content, or compliance.
02 / Practice
Connection Currency™ Tools
Eye contact. Names. Gentle tone. Cultural rituals. Daily micro-behaviors that make big safety deposits into team, classroom, and family culture.
03 / Communication
Statements Before Questions
In moments of conflict, start with grounding statements — not interrogations. People connect when they feel understood before evaluated.
04 / Response
Relational Response to Mental Health™
Behavior is communication. A five-stage protocol replacing reactive crisis management with present, emotionally aware response.

Field Studies

From seven suicides to a national framework.

Fifteen years of clinical and field practice — distilled into a framework now operationalized across organizations, communities, and entire systems.

2011

The work begins

Two decades of clinical and field practice in mental health and crisis intervention crystallize into a new question: what if the missing pillar isn't mental — it's social?

2017

The crisis

Herriman HS loses seven students to suicide in a single year. Existing mental health frameworks fail to explain the pattern.

2018

The diagnosis

Field research finds 91% of suicidal ideation traces to relationship breakdown — not clinical illness. A new category emerges: Social Health.

2020

The curriculum

Level Up launches at Herriman. Within six months: 30% drop in at-risk behavior. Within a year: ideation reports drop from 155 to 5.

2024

The framework

SHs formalizes into a Change Management System with five operational pillars and a tiered accreditation model.

2026

The movement

Accreditation launches for schools, enterprises, and healthcare. Social Health Intelligence integrated into existing systems at scale.

Meet the Founder

The framework didn't come from a whiteboard. It came from a life.

David Kozlowski Founder · LMFT

David Kozlowski didn't build Social Health Systems from theory. He built it from the inside of a depression that nearly killed him.

Raised in Carlsbad, California by his Samoan/Hawaiian grandmother — who fought for custody after his biological mother's struggles with mental health and addiction — David earned a football scholarship to the University of Utah. A series of concussions left him mentally and emotionally unstable, and in 1995 he attempted suicide.

What saved him was therapy. What followed was a 26-year career proving that the best suicide prevention is connection.

David is a Licensed Marriage and Family Therapist, the host of the OG Therapy podcast (millions of downloads), a TEDx speaker, and the architect of the first Social Health curriculum — piloted at Herriman High School in 2020 after the community lost seven youth to suicide in a single year.

The Social Health framework is the codification of that work.
