SHa Accreditation

The public signal that your culture is built on relational infrastructure.

SHa (Social Health Accreditation) is a tiered, peer-reviewed credential for organizations committed to connection-centered culture. Not a checkbox. A transformation.

W
Tier 1
White · Spark
Entry-level accreditation. Your organization has committed to Social Health and begun foundational training.
  • Social Health baseline assessment
  • Leadership introduction workshop
  • Connection Currency toolkit deployed
  • White Belt certification for 3+ leaders
P
Tier 2
Purple · Root
Mid-level accreditation. Social Health is embedded in operations, leadership practice, and communication norms.
  • All White tier requirements
  • SHs integrated into onboarding & 1:1s
  • Measurable behavior change data
  • Purple Belt certification for 10+ staff
  • Annual Social Health review cycle
B
Tier 3
Black · Legacy
Full accreditation. Social Health is the operating system of your culture — measurable, self-sustaining, and replicable.
  • All Purple tier requirements
  • Organization-wide Connection Currency metrics
  • Peer-reviewed culture assessment
  • Black Belt certifications across leadership
  • Community integration & teaching capacity

How accreditation works.

01
Apply
Submit an interest form. We'll schedule a 30-minute Social Health conversation to understand your organization.
02
Assess
A Social Health audit maps your relational ecosystem — trust, safety, communication patterns, leadership habits.
03
Implement
Your team trains in the SHs framework. Belt certifications begin. Behaviors embed into daily practice.
04
Accredit
Peer review confirms tier requirements are met. Your organization earns SHa status — publicly and internally.
